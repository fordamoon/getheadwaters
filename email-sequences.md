Hey [First Name],

A few days in — wanted to make sure you're getting everything out of your first analysis.

Here are the questions I hear most from people running it for the first time:

**"My R-squared is low. Does that mean the model isn't working?"**
Not necessarily. A low R-squared on a short dataset (under 20 weeks) just means there isn't enough variation for the model to pick up a strong signal. The fix: add more data. If you can pull 18–24 months of history, the accuracy improves significantly.

**"One of my channels shows a negative contribution. That can't be right."**
It can. Sometimes channels that are correlated with low periods (like off-season retargeting ads that run when bookings drop) show up as negative contributors. It doesn't always mean the channel is hurting you — it means the model saw it running alongside lower revenue. Worth a closer look.

**"I only have data for Facebook and Google. What about TripAdvisor?"**
Run it with what you have. Even a two-channel analysis is more useful than guessing. You can always add more channels in a second run when you have the data.

**The one thing most people miss:**
The channel contribution chart is the output that matters most. That bar chart shows you, in plain numbers, which channel drove what percentage of your revenue. Print it out. Put it next to your budget spreadsheet. That's the conversation starter.
